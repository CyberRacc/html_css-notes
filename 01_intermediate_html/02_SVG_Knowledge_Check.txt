
Knowledge Check

This section contains questions for you to check your understanding of this lesson on your own. If you’re having trouble answering a question, click it and review the material it links to.

    What is the xmlns attribute?

        Stands for XML NameSpace. It specifies what dialect of XML you're using which here
        is the SVG language spec.

        Without this, the browser will not render the image or it will render it incorrectly.

    What are some situations where you wouldn’t want to use SVG?

        When you have a very detailed image, like photorealistic images or grunge textures for
        example.

    What are the benefits of “inlining” your SVGs? What are the drawbacks?

        Inlining it means you get to use the full features of SVGs through CSS and JavaScript.
        The drawbacks is that it make your code less readable, and your page less cacheable,
        and if the SVG file is large, it will delay the loading of the HTML page.
